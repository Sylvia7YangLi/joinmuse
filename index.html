The Problem

Why swipe apps feel exhausting

Most dating apps optimize for screen time: more swipes, more chats, more noise. But people don’t want more messages. They want one good person and a real plan. And swipe culture creates anxiety: you worry if the person you like will like you back. Muse is built for the opposite outcome: fewer matches, higher intent, faster real-life connection.

Matching Engine

Mechanisms, not vibes

Weighted preference matching (宁缺毋滥)

  • You set preference weights (lifestyle, values, education, ambition, attraction, etc.).
  • Muse ranks candidates by a mutual fit score (two-way, not one-way).
  • You receive 1–3 best-fit matches/day — no endless feed.

No swipe anxiety

  • No left/right swiping loop.
  • No waiting for a “like back” just to start talking.
  • Less guessing → more confidence → more real dates.

Trust & Safety

Private accountability (no public ratings)

10-second reflection loop (private)

  • After a chat/date, users do a quick private check-in (opt-in).
  • Signals improve matching over time — we optimize for match → date conversion.

Anonymous feedback to protect good users

  • Anonymous “experience flags” for rude behavior / no-shows / unsafe conduct.
  • Repeated negative signals → down-ranked and limited.
  • Good behavior → better matches. This creates real social constraints.
  • No public score. Signals are private to avoid “score culture.”
