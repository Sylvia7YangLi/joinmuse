The problem

Why dating apps feel exhausting

Most dating apps optimize for screen time: more swipes, more chats, more noise. But people don’t want more messages. They want one good person and a real plan.

“I have 200 matches… and zero plans this weekend.”

Muse is built for the opposite outcome: fewer matches, higher intent, and faster real-life connection.

What makes Muse different

How it works

Simple user flow

Core flow

  1. Verify alumni/community and set preference weights.
  2. Get 1–3 curated matches per day — no endless feed.
  3. Chat with a 3-active-chat cap to stay focused.
  4. After a chat/date, do a 10-second reflection (private).
  5. Muse learns your preferences and improves matches over time.
Designed to optimize for dates, not dopamine.

Trust & safety (private)

  • Private trust signals, not public rankings.
  • Anonymous feedback after interactions (opt-in).
  • No “score culture”: we never show public ratings.
  • Quiet protection: repeated no-shows and bad behavior get down-ranked.
Goal: a calm, high-quality community — especially in alumni networks.

FAQ

Common questions

Is Muse only for CMU?
CMU is our starting community. The model expands to other alumni networks and cities once conversion is proven.

Why not show public ratings?
Public scores create anxiety, gaming, and unfair stigma. Muse keeps signals private to protect the community.

What do you mean by “reflection”?
A 10-second private check-in after a chat/date to improve matching and surface safety signals.

What are you optimizing for?
Match → date conversion. A “good” week is fewer chats and more real plans.
